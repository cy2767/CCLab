The Hungry Lilypad

Scientific Name:

Eateateat dapylil

Discovered by / at:

by Chae Yoon one afternoon while on a walk through Qiantan Leisure Park

Family:

The Hungry Lilypad is a close cousin of the lilypad plant that humans often see in ponds. The only difference is that the Hungry Lilypad is very hungry and also has a bit of a temper.

Habitat:

Lives in a serene pond with floating balls of light and (unfortunately) the occasional frog. All it wants in life is to eat and swim.

Appearance:

It appears to be a floating green circle with a little triangular opening through which it eats orbs of light. It floats in a seemingly random pattern, but will spin happily if patted by humans. The Hungry Lilypad turns greener the happier (and more full) it gets. If you see that the Hungry Lilypad is bright red, it means there is a frog nearby.

Key Characteristics:

  • Floats in water.
  • Eats orbs of light.
  • Dislikes (hates) frogs.
  • Changes colors based on mood.

What we know so far about the Hungry Lilypad? This Lilypad enjoys eating and is always on the hunt for food. It rotates lazily and likes the quiet life, but is easily angered when a frog enters its pond. We have not yet discovered the reason for this dislike, but rumor has it that a certain frog once trampled the Hungry Lilypad, using it as a trampoline...
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Keep the Hungry Lilypad happy. Feed it orbs of light from the pond by clicking and dragging. Entertain the Hungry Liypad by hovering over it and making it spin. Catch frogs by clicking on them to kick them out of the pond and restore peace to the Hungry Lilypad.
